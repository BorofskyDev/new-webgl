2013 | I began my career in banking and finance working for Wells Fargo in Raleigh, NC. Through hard work and great customer service, I was able to move to New York in 2016 as a Banker. While in New York I filled the roles of Banker I, Service Manager, Banker II, and Brokerage Associate (earning a Series 6 and 63). My branch was located next to one of our developer hubs. Talking to the developers about our software and how to improve it began to pique my interest in coding.

2019 | Helped found SellSafe, LLC in Wichita, KS. A Software as a Service company, we sought to develop an app that would help small businesses make better-informed credit decisions. I helped secure initial funding, created the pitch deck, and worked with developers on creating a semi-functional prototype. It’s here that I discovered an interest in UI/UX.

2020 | Due to COVID putting a halt to investments, I sought out employment that would also give me some free time to help with family in Wichita. I was hired at Whole Foods and quickly was moved to a supervisor over customer service. During that time I also began to look more into software development. I signed up for Codecademy and I realized two things: 1 – I wanted to code and 2 – I had no idea what I was doing.

2021 | I joined the coding bootcamp Coding Temple in October with the intention of going into data science. This seemed logical considering my background in finance and my love of data. However, while we only spent 3 of the 10 weeks on frontend development and 0 on UI/UX, I fell in love with frontend development. After graduating in January 2022 I knew only the bare minimum of how to code, but I knew one thing; I wanted to be a creative developer.

PRESENT | Since finishing bootcamp I’ve thrown myself into learning the basics (HTML/CSS/JS), looking over MDN documentation until the wee hours of the morning, and constantly rebuilding my own website as a way to learn. I’ve learned WebGL, Blender, React, React-Three-Fiber, and so on. While looking for work I’ve been throwing myself into learning, reading through ThreeJS’ amazing documentation, taking Bruno Simon’s Threejs-Journey course, moving away from tutorials and taking what I’ve learned and building my own stuff.

FAQ
Have any questions?

Why doesn't this site have a navbar?

It's a simple portfolio site that's meant to show off design. There's not a lot here to get through and it's easy to scroll.

Can you make a site like this for me?

My knowledge and experience are limited, but I can build out basic portfolio sites like this one.

How much does it cost to build me a site?

It just depends on what you're looking for. Adding 3D elements (like my header) will always add to the cost because of the coding involved. If you want a regular 2D site that will still look stylish (don't sleep on the CSS!) and provide all the assets (pictures, logo, etc) then the cost will be a few hundred dollars. The more work I have to do to build a site, the more you can expect to pay.

We're a small business, can you build us an ecommerce site?

At this time my skillset isn't where I feel comfortable doing this as a freelance project. I'd be more than willing to team up with an experienced developer and handle the design and front-facing aspects of the site.

"Hi, I'm with [_______] company/org/group/etc, we like your design! Can you build us a website for free? We'll give you exposure!"

No.

Why did you get into development?

I originally attended a coding bootcamp to get into data science. I learned Python, Flask, and Pandas but haven't worked with them since my bootcamp. Being able to design sites ignited something in me that only grows stronger every day.

What other coding languages do you know?

I've touched Python and Typscript. I also learned PostgreSQL and ElephantSQL. In my spare time I picked up Excel (not really a coding, but it counts for back end work).

What else do you do besides build websites and apps?

When I have the time and money I love to travel. I'll do the tourist thing for a day or two, but I prefer to spend time among the locals. My favorite place so far has been Zihuatanejo, MX, though Krakow, Poland comes close. I also play games, but mostly strategy cames and, of course, Cities: Skylines. Just something about design that attracts me to the game.
